Viktor Vasnetsov
Apocalypse
Vasnetsov, a renowned Russian artist, is best known for his works inspired by mythology, folklore, and religious themes. In "Apocalypse", he combines his masterful use of color and composition with a deeply symbolic narrative to evoke both awe and dread. The painting reflects the spiritual and moral struggles of humanity, a common theme in Vasnetsov's oeuvre.
Viktor Vasnetsov
Bogatyrs Anagoria
This monumental piece symbolizes the strength, courage, and unity of these heroic figures, who represent the ideals of justice and the defense of the motherland. Vasnetsov's attention to detail brings the bogatyrs to life, with richly detailed armor, powerful steeds, and a vast, windswept landscape that suggests the expanse of Russia.

Samolet
Viktor Vasnetsov's "Samolet" (translated as "The Flying Carpet") is a vivid and imaginative painting created in 1880. It features a young hero flying through the skies on a magical carpet, a motif drawn from Russian folklore and fairy tales. The protagonist, dressed in traditional attire, clutches a sword and gazes intently into the distance, symbolizing determination and adventure.
Viktor Vasnetsov
Crossroads
Viktor Vasnetsov's "Knight at the Crossroads" (1878) is a haunting and symbolic painting that delves into themes of choice, destiny, and the weight of heroism. It portrays a lone knight, mounted on his horse, pausing at a crossroads to contemplate his path. In front of him stands a stone inscribed with a foreboding message, warning that whichever path he chooses, danger or death awaits.
